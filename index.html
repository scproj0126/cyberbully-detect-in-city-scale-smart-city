Towards a Multi-Modal and City-Scale Approach

Identification and Detection of Cyberbullying in Social Networks within Smart Cities

Cyberbullying is a growing public-safety and wellbeing challenge in smart cities, amplified by the scale and speed of online social networks. This project investigates how machine learning models can identify harmful interactions from social media text (and, when available, other contextual signals), and compares common baseline methods with modern transformer-based approaches. Our goal is to evaluate performance, limitations, and potential improvements to support safer digital communities.
